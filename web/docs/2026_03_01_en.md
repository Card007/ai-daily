### 7. Notes on Lagrange Interpolating Polynomials

[Notes on Lagrange Interpolating Polynomials](https://eli.thegreenplace.net/2026/notes-on-lagrange-interpolating-polynomials/) — **eli.thegreenplace.net** · 03-01 02:58 · ⭐ 20/30

> Polynomial interpolation seeks the unique degree ≤ n polynomial that passes exactly through n+1 distinct data points (xi, yi). The Lagrange form builds this polynomial as a sum of basis polynomials, p(x) = Σ yi Li(x), where Li(x) = Πj≠i (x − xj)/(xi − xj) and each Li(xk) equals 1 when k=i and 0 otherwise. This representation makes existence/uniqueness and correctness almost immediate, and it also exposes how the choice of nodes (the xi values) controls numerical behavior and oscillation, not just the degree. Practical evaluation can be improved by rewriting into barycentric form to avoid recomputing products and to reduce numerical error compared with the naive O(n^2) approach. The key takeaway is that Lagrange polynomials are an elegant, constructive proof tool and a usable computational method when paired with stable evaluation techniques and well-chosen interpolation nodes.

🏷️ Lagrange, interpolation, polynomial, numerical-methods
